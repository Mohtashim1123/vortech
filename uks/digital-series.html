Entertainment Media in Pakistani: Kal aur Aaj (Past and Present )

Produced by Nabeela Aslam

Theme - Gender-sensitive Media

Funded by National Endowment for Democracy (NED), USA

Discusses the shift in the content through time - from sensitive, decent and positive content towards insensitive, derogatory, stereotypical and gender-biased content.

2016

Kahani Badal Rahi Hai (Changing Narratives)

Produced by Nabila Aslam

Theme - Gender-sensitive Media

Funded by National Endowment for Democracy (NED), USA

This short and powerful story reiterates Uks’s goals for a gender sensitive media by highlighting the blatant and subtle violations of ethics in the media in Pakistan in news, entertainment and advertisements alike. It urges the media to highlight women in positive roles by highlighting the story of Safia Khurshid, a rent-a-car business owner. Uks uncovered her story in 2008 (almost a decade ago) through one of its radio series. Nabila Aslam does a follow-up on her story explaining how not only has she survived but her business has flourished in this so-called male domain. The mainstream media needs to bring out more such success stories of women and to bust gender stereotypes.

2015

Energy for People

Produced by Nabila Aslam

Theme - Alternate Energy Sources – Energy journalism

Funded by Shell

A series of five interesting stories of women and men, from rural areas of Pakistan including Thatta and Kot Addu, depicting how people in the far-flung areas are innovatively using energy resources to meet their needs.

The stories include :

  • Alternate energy (Biogas) initiatives by industrialists
  • Natural gas load-shedding impact onlivelihoods, Story of Tasleem, a tailor.
  • Case study of Rukhsana Shoaib - a woman who changes engine oil at a workshop in Kot Addu,Sindh running a grocery store and oil change shop.
2014

SAVE - Mother Schools

Produced by Nabila Aslam

Theme -Countering Violent Extremism

Funded by Sister Against Violent Extremism (SAVE)

A digital story on views and reviews of ‘Mothers’ who received training on how to recognize early signs or inclination towards religious extremism in their sons in combatting violent extremism.
