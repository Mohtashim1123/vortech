Tawanae Khushali Aur Hum (Energy, Development and Us)

Produced by Sadia Widad

Theme - Energy journalism (filter: gender-sensitive media

Language - Urdu

Programmes - 5 Radio Programmes

Funded by Shell, Pakistan

A radio series on how energy can empower women. This series brought forward stories from remote areas of Sindh, Pakistan and the impact of energy crises on home-based workers, small-business owners, transporters and general public.This series was produced to serve as an example for the media practitioners to build awareness about energy shortageand to contribute to the development of a wider body of young and enthusiasticPakistani journalists who not only understand energy-relatedissues, but also are able to analyseissues outside the realm of high-level policy movements and critical impacts, and contribute to sustainable energy solutions through their reporting. The aim was not to report news but to develop a research-based attitude to reporting on energy, specially through the gender lens,that has critical impact not only on the lives of masses but also has economic and developmental reverberations on the country.

Indraj Hoga tu Naam Hoga(Registration would bring recognition)

Produced by ---

Theme - Legal rights(filter: legal rights)

Language - Urdu

Programmes - 16

Funded by Adetude Agency

A radio series on importanceof registration of children at birth.It is the fundamental right of a child to get aregistrationcertificate from the place of origin.The denial of this right to legal recognition, feeds into a vicious cycle of rejection and exclusion of a child from other guaranteed rights, protections and benefits. The absence of a birth certificate, in several cases, means the denial of an education and inaccessibility to free healthcare, which then come to have a direct bearing on a child’s future economic prospects and the quality of his life. Without proof of age, a child may be denied the minimum protections provided under our law against child labour and, as seen in several cases, against child marriages.Non-registration of births is a problem of lack of awareness and also accessibility which this radio series hopes to address

Aawaz Hamaari Qoo’at (Our Voice, our Strength)

Produced by Production team from PANOS, Saadia Haq

Theme - Women’s Empowerment(filter: women’s empowerment)

Language - Urdu

Programmes - 10

Funded by PANOS

Of women, for women’ and most importantly ‘by women’ –this is how one can describe this radio programmes launched a day before the International Women’s Day 2011. Featuring the voices of young women from Lyari on issues that are important to their lives –girls’ education, women’s health, pollution, the impact of drug addiction, and early marriages, Lyari’s young women speak out. This dynamic group of 30 young women were trained how to use voice recorders to record interviews, voxpops, oral testimonies and discussions with women, young and old -and a few cooperative men -from their neighbourhood. The young women between 15 –25 years of age,focusedon their local issues in an attempt to bring about behaviouralchanges in the girls, the locality in particular and listeners in general.

Theme - Disability (filter : human rights)

Language - Urdu

Programmes - 10

Funded by Netherlands Embassy

Persons with disabilities are not regularly seen on media, and when they are seen or heard, it’s mostly about disability issues. They are rarely portrayed as person with opinions on news and topical issues. This radio series on the rights of the disabled and the responsibilities of society towards them and put emphases on the need to understand and accept the abilities, not disabilities of the disabled persons.The radio series portrays persons with disabilities as active participants of societyto shape their public image. Interacting and positive portrayal in social and work environments helps break down barriers and opens up lines of communicationand was the aim of this radio series.
